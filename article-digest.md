## 1. Cutting unplanned downtime across enterprise SaaS
**Track:** strategy | **Competencies:** data-informed decisions, product impact, research-led design

- **Situation:** Enterprise SaaS products at IBM carried high unplanned downtime that hurt reliability and customer trust.
- **Task:** Reduce downtime by improving the workflows where failures and delays originated.
- **Action:** Ran user research, folded in machine-learning insights, redesigned the core workflows, and validated each change against a hypothesis rather than shipping on instinct.
- **Result:** Cut unplanned downtime 43% across the affected products.
- **Use when:** the question is about measurable impact, working with data, or turning a reliability problem into a design problem.

## 2. Finding $2M of new product opportunity through research
**Track:** strategy | **Competencies:** discovery, product sense, customer insight

- **Situation:** The portfolio had unmet customer needs that weren't visible in the existing roadmap.
- **Task:** Surface real opportunities the business could act on.
- **Action:** Led generative and evaluative research, then translated the findings into prioritized, testable product concepts the team could build against.
- **Result:** Generated $2M in new product opportunity.
- **Use when:** the question is about customer obsession, discovery, or influencing what gets built.

## 3. Scaling and developing a 12-person design team
**Track:** design_leadership | **Competencies:** people leadership, mentorship, raising craft

- **Situation:** At IBM I led 12 designers across 7 products, with uneven delivery quality between teams.
- **Task:** Lift consistency and grow the designers without taking the pen out of their hands.
- **Action:** Established critique rituals, set craft standards, and gave designers at different levels the direction and decision frameworks to make stronger calls on their own.
- **Result:** Raised delivery consistency across the products and developed designers at a range of levels.
- **Use when:** the question is about managing or growing a team, developing people, or setting a quality bar.

## 4. Building design systems that moved a business deal
**Track:** people_leadership | **Competencies:** systems thinking, craft, speed to market

- **Situation:** Client programs at Publicis Sapient needed faster, more consistent delivery to compete.
- **Task:** Reduce rework and speed up the path from design to shipped product.
- **Action:** Launched 3 scalable design systems with reusable components, design tokens, and governance, and coached 5 designers through Agile and Lean UX.
- **Result:** Improved speed to market, contributed to a $10M deal, and earned the 1st Place Customer Experience Award in June 2019.
- **Use when:** the question is about design systems, cross-functional delivery, or connecting craft to business outcomes.

## 5. Simplifying a workflow from 3 days to 2.5 hours
**Track:** strategy | **Competencies:** execution, ambiguity, emerging tech

- **Situation:** A core user task at Publicis Sapient took 3 days and blocked downstream work.
- **Task:** Strip the friction out of a genuinely complex workflow.
- **Action:** Designed an emerging-technology solution as a hands-on lead, working closely with engineering on what was feasible.
- **Result:** Cut the task from 3 days to 2.5 hours.
- **Use when:** the question is about handling complexity, hands-on problem solving, or 10x improvements.

## 6. Modernizing IRS financial systems under heavy constraint
**Track:** hybrid | **Competencies:** influence without authority, accessibility, regulated domains

- **Situation:** Two mission-critical IRS systems supporting over $1 trillion in annual tax collections needed modernization under dense financial, legal, and compliance rules.
- **Task:** Set design direction and influence the roadmap without owning the team.
- **Action:** Translated compliance requirements into scalable interaction patterns and implementation-ready specs, delivered Section 508 and WCAG-compliant experiences, and synthesized research and constraints into prioritized recommendations for senior leadership.
- **Result:** Shaped design direction across systems operating at national scale and influenced roadmap priorities without direct authority.
- **Use when:** the question is about stakeholder influence, working in regulated or high-stakes environments, or accessibility.
