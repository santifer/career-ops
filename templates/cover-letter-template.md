## Structure (4 blocks)

```
[HOOK — earns attention in sentence one]

Sentence one is a specific observation about the company or role that no other applicant has the proof to make. Not "I'm excited about Anthropic" — that signals nothing. Instead: "The Anthropic Communications Manager, Research role asks for someone who can translate research output for a sophisticated audience under measurement discipline. I've spent eight years doing that for live broadcast, and the past two doing it for the production AI agents serving 1,000+ senior engineers at Google."

[PROOF — one or two STAR+R proof points with cv.md citation]

Pick from `article-digest.md` (proof points #1–#18). For Anthropic-shape roles, lead with #17 (AJ+ talent pipeline). For pure-A2 roles, lead with #1 (Communications Triage Agent) or #2 (Voice DNA / RAG pipeline). For pure-B roles, lead with the strongest editorial-discipline proof (PrEP/Truvada credit, Mandela 44-min special, Ahmed coalition).

State the proof in 2-3 sentences. Include the metric and the source citation in your head (don't write [cv.md:L33] in the final letter — that's for verification, not the recruiter's read).

[DIFFERENTIATOR — why your unique combination matters here]

One sentence on the broadcast + AI angle. Not in those words — in the words that map to the role.

For Anthropic Communications Manager Research:
> "Live-broadcast-grade editorial discipline applied to AI agent systems is rare. Most candidates bring one side or the other — I built and shipped both."

For Sierra DRE / Forward Deployed:
> "I've shipped four production AI agents at Google including a three-prompt classification system serving 1,000+ senior engineering ICs. I've also field-produced live broadcast under crisis (Hong Kong Umbrella Revolution; Hurricane Maria with the San Juan Mayor on camera). Forward deployed work demands both."

For OpenAI Communications:
> "OpenAI's research-side communications operates under regulatory and reputational scrutiny. I've spent the past 16 years operating in environments where every output carried legal, political, or safety exposure — Scientology defectors at HuffPost Live, presidential-cycle coverage during the active $500M Trump-Univision lawsuit at Fusion, the producer coalition that recently helped free Ahmed Shihab-Eldin from Kuwaiti detention."

[CTA — specific, low-friction ask]

Not "I'd love the opportunity to chat." Instead, something that makes saying yes feel obvious:

For cold:
> "If the role is still open, I'd value 15 minutes to walk through the talent-pipeline pattern at AJ+ — three producers I coached became on-air principals at one of the largest digital news properties of the era, and the editorial-discipline-as-systems-design framing maps directly to what your JD describes."

For warm intro / referred:
> "Per [referrer's name]'s suggestion. Happy to send a tailored two-pager covering the Communications Triage Agent architecture and the Voice DNA / Kill List methodology if that helps the read."

For follow-up after LinkedIn DM:
> "Following our exchange — attaching the formal application here. The Voice DNA / Kill List proof point is on page 1 of the CV."
```

## Voice profile constraints (always-on)

Per `corpus/voice-profile.md`:

- **Lead with the point.** Sentence one carries the load. Don't bury the lede.
- **Questions as relationship maintenance.** OK to ask, sparingly.
- **Specific warmth.** "Three producers I coached" beats "I love mentoring."
- **Contractions in casual.** Cover letters are professional → contractions out.
- **Tonal shifts on errors.** If the JD has a typo or wrong info, mention it neutrally.
- **Bumping-back-up follow-up pattern.** Keep this in mind for the CTA — make replying feel obvious.

**Banned phrases** (sample from voice-profile.md — full list there is canonical):
- "I'm excited to..."
- "I'm passionate about..."
- "It would be an honor..."
- "I would be a great fit because..."
- Any clause beginning with "Furthermore"
- Any clause beginning with "Moreover"
- Any sentence that ends with "looking forward to hearing from you"

**Compression test:**
- Cut every adjective you can lose
- Cut every "I" you can replace with action
- Cut every "very" / "really" / "quite"
- If the cut version reads better, ship the cut

**Spelling and small things:**
- "yeah" not "yea" (matters in casual / DM contexts; cover letter avoids both)
- Em dash (—) for parenthetical clauses — single space on each side, U+2014, not double-hyphen
- Tabular numerics for any digit cluster (160 hrs / 99% / 50M+)

```bash

```
