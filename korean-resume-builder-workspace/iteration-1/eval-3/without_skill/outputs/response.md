## Understanding Korean Resume Conventions

### Key Differences from English CV Format

A proper Korean resume (이력서) has specific formatting expectations:

1. **Personal Information Section (상단)**
   - Full legal name (in Korean, ideally)
   - Date of birth or age
   - Contact info (phone + email)
   - Address in Korea (if applicable)
   - Photo (often included, professional headshot)

2. **Standard Section Order** (differs from English CV)
   - Personal information (맨 위)
   - Education (학력)
   - Work experience (경력)
   - Skills (기술)
   - Certifications/Additional (자격증 및 기타)

3. **Language & Tone**
   - Formal but not stuffy (존댓말 or neutral formal Korean)
   - Third-person references are common
   - Action verbs often used differently than English
   - Metrics and outcomes emphasized (like English, but with Korean business context)

4. **Chronological vs. Reverse Chronological**
   - Typically reverse chronological (most recent first)
   - Education section often includes "graduation expected" dates
   - Gaps explained naturally

5. **Company Context**
   - Korean companies want to see progression within organizations
   - 직급 (job level/title) progression matters
   - Entrepreneurship and side projects are increasingly valued (showing agency)

### Self-Introduction Letter (자기소개서)

If Naver requests a cover letter equivalent, they'll likely ask for a 자기소개서 (self-introduction letter), not a traditional cover letter. This is different:

- **자기소개서:** Personal story + why this company/role + how you fit their culture
- **Cover Letter (English):** Job-specific fit + enthusiasm + call to action

This is typically required for competitive Korean positions.
