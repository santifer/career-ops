## OpenAI — Equity Deep Research (2026-05-10, Gemini 2.5 Pro grounded)

**Equity / IPO posture:** Late-stage / $122B @ $852B valuation, March 2026 / IPO window 12-24mo+ / grant style is now RSU for new hires / no public refresher cadence / gap not public / no recent tender or secondary reported. (confidence: H)

**Overpay signal:** OpenAI's stock-based compensation averaged ~$1.5 million per employee in 2025, the highest of any tech startup in history and representing 46% of revenue. (confidence: H)

**Desperate-hire signal:** In March 2026, OpenAI announced plans to nearly double its workforce from ~4,500 to 8,000 employees by the end of 2026, focusing on product, engineering, research, and sales to compete with enterprise rivals. (confidence: H)

**Tactical lead this week:** Reference their early May 2026 expansion of the ChatGPT ads platform, including the new self-serve Ads Manager and partnerships with major holding companies like Dentsu and WPP, and ask how the Media Partnerships team is supporting these new enterprise advertisers.

**Grant-instrument detail (Gemini's specific value-add):** As of early 2026, OpenAI has transitioned from issuing Profit Participation Units (PPUs) to Restricted Stock Units (RSUs) for all new offers, signaling a move to a more conventional, scalable compensation structure ahead of a potential IPO. PPUs were a contractual right to a capped share of future profits, not direct equity. The shift to RSUs simplifies compensation and makes offers more legible against the market. An exact 409A-to-preferred discount is not public, nor is the RSU trigger structure.
