## EDITORIAL LEAD-TIME VERIFICATION

**Status:** ✅ VERIFIED — all three dates confirmed

| Story | HuffPost Live Coverage | Mainstream/Policy Date | Lead Time |
|-------|----------------------|----------------------|-----------|
| Jazz Jennings / trans youth | HuffPost Live (2012–2013) | TLC "I Am Jazz" premiere: **July 15, 2015** | ~2–3 years |
| Transgender military service | HuffPost Live panel (2012–2013) | Pentagon ban lifted: **June 30, 2016** (Sec. Def. Ash Carter) | ~3–4 years |
| PrEP / Truvada for HIV prevention | HuffPost Live (2013) | FDA approved: **July 16, 2012**; mainstream uptake/coverage began: **fall 2013** | Mitchell's coverage was contemporaneous with early mainstream wave |

**Sources:**
- TLC "I Am Jazz" premiere: https://realscreen.com/2015/06/02/tlcs-i-am-jazz-to-premiere-on-july-15/ and IMDB https://www.imdb.com/title/tt4523638/
- Pentagon transgender ban lifted June 30, 2016: https://www.cnn.com/2016/06/30/politics/transgender-ban-lifted-us-military/index.html
- PrEP/Truvada FDA approval July 16, 2012: Gilead Sciences press release https://www.gilead.com/news/news-details/2012/us-food-and-drug-administration-approves-gileads-truvada-for-reducing-the-risk-of-acquiring-hiv
- PrEP mainstream coverage timeline: Media Matters (May 2014) documents media underreporting. POZ magazine notes mainstream uptake began climbing from fall 2013.

**PrEP framing note:** The FDA approved Truvada for PrEP on July 16, 2012. Mainstream media largely ignored it until fall 2013. HuffPost Live coverage in 2013 was therefore on the early wave of mainstream attention — ahead of most outlets but not ahead of the FDA approval itself. The claim "6 months before mainstream" is plausible for 2013 HuffPost Live coverage if the mainstream wave is dated to late 2013/early 2014. This should be framed carefully: "when PrEP was still being ignored by most mainstream media outlets" rather than a precise lead-time figure.
