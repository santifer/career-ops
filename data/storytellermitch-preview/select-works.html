02

AJE / The Stream — Bin Laden Night Launch (May 2, 2011)

250M households. Eight social platforms integrated live. RTS Most Innovative Programme (2012).

The launch broadcast for Al Jazeera English's The Stream was planned for three stories. It aired on the night Bin Laden was killed. As a founding-team AP, I was part of the team that pivoted overnight — "We had three stories and... we had to switch everything around" — booking Sohaib Athar, the Abbottabad man who had live-tweeted the raid, live on Skype, while integrating eight social platforms simultaneously for a 250-million-household audience. His follower count was graphed live on-air in real time: 751 (April 30) → 32,000 (May 1) → 59,000 (May 2).

During the broadcast: Mariam Al-Khawaja reported her father, two brothers-in-law, and uncle had been jailed by the Bahraini government — live, on-air. In the post-show: Matar Matar, a Bahraini opposition MP, was arrested by masked men in civilian vehicles while we were still live.

This is where I learned what real-time information systems under pressure actually require.

Downstream@ReallyVirtual's accidental documentation is now cited in journalism textbooks as a landmark in citizen journalism. The Stream won the RTS Most Innovative Programme Award, a Webby Award, and received an Emmy nomination.
SourceAl Jazeera English · 250M household global reach · Eight-platform simultaneous integration · RTS Most Innovative Programme; Webby; Emmy nomination

AJ+ — Measles Outbreaks USA (2017)

50M views on Facebook. Two minutes. One editorial position.

Fifty million views. Two minutes. One clear editorial position: the measles outbreak was a vaccination failure, not a debate. Most outlets were hedging. This video did not. Data-driven (94% of Minnesota cases among unvaccinated individuals, herd immunity mechanics explained in under a minute), direct framing, urgent news peg. The lesson for technical communicators: precision and confidence in the framing is not optional when the facts are clear.

DownstreamMinnesota's 2017 outbreak — primarily in the Somali-American community targeted by anti-vax misinformation — became a landmark case study in public health communication failure. Cited in the 2019 federal measles emergency declaration.
SourceAJ+ Facebook · 50M views · 500M+ weekly platform audience

Fusion — Mandela Breaking News Special (December 5, 2013)

44-minute primetime broadcast. Three-way ABC / Univision / Fusion integration on a rebuilt rundown.

The rundown was built for a different night. When Mandela died at 8:50 PM local time on December 5, 2013, everything was rebuilt from scratch — and the broadcast integrated ABC News packages (Karen Travers, Jim Avila), Obama White House audio in full, Jacob Zuma's official South African government statement, four consecutive live phone experts (Paul Danzil of the Truth and Reconciliation Commission; Derek Ashong; Zarina Patel, documentary filmmaker and NGO founder; Jim Avila), a Twitter reaction segment, and archival footage — all within 44 minutes of live primetime television. The broadcast closed with Mandela's 1990 prison-release speech.

For anyone who has managed a multi-stakeholder product launch under changing conditions: this is the same problem set.

AnchorMariana Atencio (solo). ABC co-anchor: Lindsay Janis in studio.

HuffPost Live — PrEP / Truvada Panel (November 8, 2012)

Six months ahead of mainstream coverage. Host credited me on tape: "who brought this to our attention."

This is what story origination looks like: a 29-minute live expert panel on HIV pre-exposure prophylaxis, produced roughly six months after FDA approval and well ahead of mainstream coverage. The IPREX clinical trial chair, Robert Grant, joined live from Lima, Peru. The AIDS Healthcare Foundation's chief of public affairs took the opposing position. The tension was real — this was not a consensus segment.

The distinction between producing a segment and identifying why it matters is the whole point.

"Thank you to our wonderful producer, Mitchell Williams, who brought this to our attention." — host, [29:00]

DownstreamPrEP was FDA-approved in July 2012 but clinically invisible in the communities it served. By 2014, the CDC was recommending it for all high-risk individuals. Early media coverage was part of that shift.

HuffPost Live — Trans Military Panel (September 25, 2012 — 31 min)

Four years before the 2016 Pentagon policy reversal.

Produced four years before the June 2016 Pentagon reversal on transgender military service. Panel: Bryn Tanhill (trans Navy pilot), Monica Helms (TAVA, Navy submarine vet 1970–78), David McKean (SLDN Legal Director), Sue Fulton (OutServe), and "Olivia" — an anonymous active-duty Navy service member who participated at genuine discharge risk. Host: Marc Lamont Hill.

The question on the table was whether trans people could serve openly. The Pentagon's answer, four years later, was yes. This panel named the arguments before the policy debate was formally joined.

DownstreamPentagon reversed transgender military policy June 2016. The 2017 reversal and subsequent litigation drew directly on the advocacy infrastructure built in this period.
SourceHuffPost Live · Originally broadcast HuffPost Live; archived by AJ+ in 2017 during the Trump ban controversy.

HuffPost Live — Jazz / Trans Youth (April 2, 2013)

58-minute live episode. Two years before TLC's I Am Jazz.

Two years before Jazz became a television personality, I produced this 58-minute live full episode with her as the anchor guest, alongside Dr. Maddie Deutsch of UCSF's Center for Excellence for Transgender Health — who disclosed her own trans identity on air during the broadcast. For technical audiences: the skill here is recognizing a signal before the algorithm confirms it.
