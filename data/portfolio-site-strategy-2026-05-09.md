## Section 9 — Voice and Tone Calibration

### Register (cross-page)
- **Lead with the point.** First sentence of every page should answer "what does this person do" or "what is this page about" in 12 words or fewer.
- **Specific warmth.** Name people, dates, timestamps. "PrEP panel, Nov 8 2012, host credited me at [29:00]" beats "produced HIV-prevention content."
- **No marketing throat-clearing.** Cut "Welcome to my portfolio." Cut "My journey began..." Cut "I'm excited to share."
- **Sentence length variation.** Voice corpus shows Mitchell uses long sentences with embedded clauses for setup, then short sentences for landing. *Reference Bin Laden Night paragraph in current rewrites — that pattern.*
- **Contractions in casual register.** "I'm" / "didn't" / "won't" — but not in the hero positioning line, which gets full formal weight.
- **No emojis on the public-facing site.** Per voice profile + general professional default.
- **Spelling:** "yeah" not "yea" if any informal voice surfaces.

### Tone (audience-calibrated)

The site has to land for two audiences simultaneously:

- **Technical / builder hiring audience** (Anthropic AI Solutions Architect recruiter, OpenAI FDE hiring manager, Sierra ML lead, xAI engineering recruiter): cares about *production scale + measured outcomes + architectural specificity*. Will read the Build page first.
- **Editorial / comms hiring audience** (Anthropic Editorial AI for Science, Atlantic AI vertical, Substack editorial, Axios Smart Brevity adjacent): cares about *editorial judgment + on-tape credibility + voice consistency*. Will read About and Select Works first.

**The voice has to be one voice across both audiences, not code-switched.** Per the 2026 research: "Big Tech is willing to pay a premium for professionals who can cut through the noise and build trust" specifically because AI-flooded content has commodified generic voice. Mitchell's editorial register is the differentiator; do not soften it for the technical audience.

### Compression test
Every paragraph on the live site must survive a 40% cut without losing what makes it Mitchell's. If a paragraph survives the cut and reads better cut, ship the cut version.
