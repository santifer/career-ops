# Persuasion Frameworks

**Cialdini's 6 principles of influence + Fogg Behavior Model applied to operational-dashboard emails.**

## Why it matters for operational-dashboard emails

Operational emails are NOT marketing emails — they optimize for return-to-known-state and action-conversion in the context of an urgent or routine task. But they still leverage human psychology. Cialdini's Reciprocity (offering clarity first), Commitment-Consistency (reinforcing the user's goals), and Authority (showing system status) directly increase the likelihood that a reader acts on the email. The Fogg Behavior Model makes this concrete: **Behavior = Motivation × Ability × Trigger**. The email is the Trigger. Ability is the button affordance. Motivation is the urgency + runway state.

## Tactical applications

1. **Reciprocity**: Lead with clarity. Heartbeat email shows the user "here's what happened since yesterday" before asking for action. No hidden agenda. Users reciprocate by opening and clicking.

2. **Commitment-Consistency**: Align the email message with known user goals (e.g., "you've reviewed 12/40 roles; 28 to go"). Users who see themselves as goal-pursuing tend to continue pursuing.

3. **Authority**: Surface system status with confidence. "Scan completed: 47 new postings" is more persuasive than "There might be postings available." The signal of system function is itself authoritative.

4. **Fogg's Ability lever**: Make the primary CTA button large, clear, high-contrast, in known position (top-right, or top-center below headline). Reduce friction: if the user must hunt for the button, they won't click.

## Common failure mode

Ops emails that over-bury the action under layers of context (system status, metrics, historical data) forget that Motivation is finite. A reader's motivation to act degrades with every paragraph they must parse. If the primary action is below the fold AND below 3+ paragraphs of status, the Trigger is too late in the sequence. Analyzer spots this by measuring: distance from headline to primary CTA button (should be <200px on mobile).
