## How it works

1. Every time `/career-ops oferta` generates Block F (Interview Plan), new STAR+R stories get appended here
2. Before your next interview, review this file — your stories are already organized by theme
3. The "Big Three" questions can be answered with stories from this bank:
   - "Tell me about yourself" → combine 2-3 stories into a narrative
   - "Tell me about your most impactful project" → pick your highest-impact story
   - "Tell me about a conflict you resolved" → find a story with a Reflection

### [Network / Crisis Comms / OPSEC] Ahmed Shihab-Eldin Coalition (April 2026)
**Source:** article-digest.md #18 — Proof point added Stage 6 Re-Open v2

**S (Situation):** Ahmed Shihab-Eldin — former HuffPost Live co-host, AJ+ contributor, one of the most prominent Arab-American journalists of the 2010s digital era — was detained in Kuwait beginning early March 2026 on charges the international press-freedom community treated as politically motivated. By mid-April he had been held 52 days without resolution. CPJ went public April 13. CNN broke the broader story April 14, noting "many of those who worked with Ahmed... have banded together in recent days to sound the alarm."

**T (Task):** Contribute to the producer-side coalition forming around CPJ's #FreeAhmed campaign — amplifying the case across professional and personal networks under OPSEC discipline (Signal-coordinated given the detention context), without becoming a vector for additional risk to Ahmed or his legal team.

**A (Action):** Joined the coalition formed primarily from former HuffPost Live and AJ+ producer networks. Coordinated via Signal with peer producers and CPJ-aligned channels to amplify the #FreeAhmed campaign through US and international networks. Worked alongside CPJ (Jodie Ginsberg), IFJ, Amnesty, Gulf Centre for Human Rights, UN Special Rapporteur Francesca Albanese, and international legal counsel led by Caoilfhionn Gallagher KC and Kate Gibson. Public-facing coalition work included Caroline Modarressy-Tehrani's MS NOW opinion piece (April 16) and Jacob Soboroff's Instagram amplification — participated operationally with discretion appropriate to the case's risk profile.

**R (Result):** Ahmed acquitted on all charges April 23, released April 25, 2026 — 11 days from CPJ going public to release. Four capabilities demonstrated: (1) sustained network operational a decade after the underlying employer shut down; (2) multi-stakeholder international pressure campaign spanning CPJ, US government channels, Kuwaiti channels, and international legal counsel; (3) OPSEC discipline — no operational details surfaced publicly that could compromise Ahmed or coalition members; (4) crisis comms architecture under time pressure with measurable outcome.

**Reflection:** A media-era network doesn't atrophy just because the employer closes — it stays alive if you invest in it. The discipline of Signal-coordinating a sensitive campaign without leaking anything operationally useful is the same discipline required for AI communications work under regulatory scrutiny. The outcome was measurable and public.

**Best for questions about:**
- "Tell me about a time you worked across a complex multi-stakeholder environment"
- "Tell me about a time you operated under pressure with high stakes"
- "How do you maintain relationships outside your current role?"
- "Tell me about a time you had to exercise discretion / navigate sensitive information"
- "What does your network look like in [journalism / media]?" (media partnerships roles)
- Any question probing whether the journalism experience is current or historical
