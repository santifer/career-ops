## Story 1: Team Promotions Pipeline
**Theme:** Coaching, developing engineers
**Best for:** "Building a high-performing team" / "Developing engineers"
- **S:** EM at Ocado, team of 6, no structured progression framework
- **T:** Build a coaching model that produces measurable career growth
- **A:** Structured 1:1s with explicit skill laddering; one business-impacting stretch project per engineer per quarter; transparent tracking
- **R:** Promotions to Senior, Staff, Senior Staff within 3 years. 100% rotation graduate retention.
- **Reflection:** Career growth requires explicit conversations about what good looks like at the next level. "What does the level above you do that you don't do yet?"

## Story 2: 14-Person Cross-Team Delivery
**Theme:** Delivery accountability, managing without authority
**Best for:** "Complex project delivered" / "Managed without direct authority"
- **S:** Cross-functional project — data scientists, analysts, engineers across multiple teams, 14 contributors
- **T:** Deliver on time without direct line management over most contributors
- **A:** Automated blocker visibility on scrum board; surfaced dependencies early; maintained shared capacity view
- **R:** Delivered on schedule; inter-team secondments and knowledge-sharing as by-product
- **Reflection:** Accountability without authority requires information systems, not just relationships.

## Story 3: Cycle Time Improvement
**Theme:** Agile delivery, process improvement
**Best for:** "Improved delivery process" / "Changed how the team worked"
- **S:** Sprint cycles too long, WIP sprawling, low sense of progress
- **T:** Improve delivery cadence without breaking morale
- **A:** Reduced sprint length; capped WIP; introduced just-in-time technical discovery
- **R:** 30% cycle time improvement; team reported clearer focus
- **Reflection:** People confuse busyness with progress.

## Story 4: Department-Wide UI Working Group
**Theme:** Engineering standards, cross-org influence
**Best for:** "Drove adoption of standards" / "Influenced beyond your team"
- **S:** UI technical debt owned by 1 team; 5 others contributing without accountability
- **T:** Extend ownership without top-down mandate
- **A:** Built cross-team working group; defined practices collaboratively; ran dissemination forum every 3 weeks
- **R:** Ownership from 1 team to 6; practices codified and adopted department-wide
- **Reflection:** Standards spread through communities of practice, not mandates.

## Story 5: AWS Lambda Migration
**Theme:** Technical decision-making, reliability, cost
**Best for:** "Significant technical decision" / "Improved reliability"
- **S:** High-volume forecasting system — production incidents, high hosting cost
- **T:** Reduce incidents and cost; build stakeholder buy-in before committing
- **A:** Built simulation data first; presented evidence-based business case; zero-incident cutover
- **R:** Production incidents eliminated; 85% cost reduction; 5× faster
- **Reflection:** Build the evidence before asking for the green light.

## Story 6: Modular Monolith Architecture
**Theme:** Architecture decision, greenfield
**Best for:** "Architecture decision" / "Balancing speed vs quality"
- **S:** Greenfield project under delivery pressure; risk of architectural debt
- **T:** Enable speed now with a clean path to microservices later
- **A:** Proposed modular monolith; enforced module boundaries via architecture testing; documented split criteria
- **R:** Team shipped fast; clean separation maintained
- **Reflection:** Premature microservices kill early-stage velocity. Architecture tests kept it honest.

## Story 7: SLO + Monitoring Before Go-Live
**Theme:** Reliability, DevOps mindset, quality culture
**Best for:** "Improved reliability" / "You build it, you run it"
- **S:** New warehouse orchestration service going live; no observability, no runbooks
- **T:** Production-ready before launch, not after the first incident
- **A:** Defined SLOs; implemented CloudWatch + PagerDuty; wrote support playbook; made it part of definition of done
- **R:** Zero incidents at launch; on-call team had clear procedures
- **Reflection:** Monitoring and runbooks are part of the definition of done.

## Story 8: PM/UX/Eng Alignment
**Theme:** Cross-functional collaboration, stakeholder management
**Best for:** "Working with non-engineering stakeholders"
- **S:** Engineering was a gatekeeper, not a design partner; late-stage descoping was common
- **T:** Reposition EM as part of discovery, not just delivery
- **A:** Set up feasibility reviews before roadmap lock; attended discovery sessions
- **R:** Reduced late-stage descoping; EM included in discovery as standard
- **Reflection:** Surface constraints before commitments are made, not after.

## Story 9: Partner Migration — Zero Incidents
**Theme:** High-stakes delivery, risk management, stakeholder comms
**Best for:** "High-pressure delivery" / "Keeping stakeholders informed"
- **S:** Migrating live retail partners to new ordering system — real business consequences
- **T:** Zero disruption to partner operations
- **A:** Used simulation data to pre-sell the plan; kept stakeholders informed throughout; rehearsed rollback
- **R:** Zero incidents; trust maintained; retailer bought in based on data
- **Reflection:** Trust is earned by showing work, not by reassuring people.

## Story 10: Automation to Reduce Management Toil
**Theme:** Automation mindset, EM productivity
**Best for:** "Making your team more effective" / "Innovation in how you manage"
- **S:** Timesheet lag 28 days average; security issues tracked manually with no enforcement
- **T:** Eliminate recurring management toil
- **A:** Built AI-powered automations: timesheet reminders; security tracker with SLA enforcement; scrum blocker signaling
- **R:** Timesheet lag 28 days → 10 days company-wide; security issues resolved within SLAs
- **Reflection:** An EM chasing timesheets isn't coaching anyone.
