# Story Bank — Master STAR+R Stories

This file accumulates your best interview stories over time. Each evaluation (Block F) adds new stories here. Instead of memorizing 100 answers, maintain 5-10 deep stories that you can bend to answer almost any behavioral question.

## How it works

1. Every time `/career-ops oferta` generates Block F (Interview Plan), new STAR+R stories get appended here
2. Before your next interview, review this file — your stories are already organized by theme
3. The "Big Three" questions can be answered with stories from this bank:
   - "Tell me about yourself" → combine 2-3 stories into a narrative
   - "Tell me about your most impactful project" → pick your highest-impact story
   - "Tell me about a conflict you resolved" → find a story with a Reflection

### [Confidentiality] The Workers Comp File
**Source:** Report #002 — Queensland Parliament — Assistant Electorate Officer
**S:** Occupational health clinic (4cRisk) managing injury and health records across multiple client companies, including highly sensitive workers compensation cases.
**T:** Maintain records in strict compliance with privacy law while liaising with insurers and medical providers, without disclosing health information to line management.
**A:** Built systematic filing protocols with separate access controls for health versus administrative records; trained colleagues on privacy obligations and escalation procedures.
**R:** Zero privacy breaches across two years; records audit-ready at all times.
**Reflection:** Discretion is a system, not just a personality trait — it requires structure, not intent. I learned to build the system so that good outcomes don't depend on individuals having a good day.
**Best for questions about:** handling sensitive or confidential information; privacy compliance; working with vulnerable people; discretion under pressure.

### [Competing Priorities] The 150-Worker Office
**Source:** Report #002 — Queensland Parliament — Assistant Electorate Officer
**S:** Single administrative office supporting a workforce of 150+ across multiple project sites (Thiess/TrackStar Alliance), staffed by two permanent administrators.
**T:** Simultaneously manage inductions, compliance training tracking, procurement, safety documentation and workforce records during a construction delivery peak.
**A:** Developed a daily priority triage system; prepared reusable templates for recurring tasks; communicated proactively with site supervisors about realistic timelines.
**R:** Office ran without incident through the peak delivery period; zero missed compliance deadlines.
**Reflection:** A small team only functions if every person owns their area completely. I learned to work as if I were the last line of defence on every task.
**Best for questions about:** competing priorities; working in a small team; managing without supervision; high-volume administration.

### [Initiative / Process Improvement] The SMS System
**Source:** Report #002 — Queensland Parliament — Assistant Electorate Officer
**S:** Safety incident notification at Thiess project sites relied on phone calls and paper forms; average notification time from incident to management was 45 minutes.
**T:** Design a faster process within existing technology and budget constraints.
**A:** Identified that workers had mobile phones on-site; built an SMS-based reporting chain that simultaneously notified site management, safety, and HR at the moment of report.
**R:** Average notification time fell from 45 minutes to under 10 minutes; system was adopted across multiple Thiess project sites.
**Reflection:** The best improvements are invisible to users — they just notice things work better. I would involve end users earlier in the testing phase next time to reduce adoption friction.
**Best for questions about:** process improvement; initiative without being asked; working within constraints; measurable impact.

### [Stakeholder Liaison] The Insurer Maze
**Source:** Report #002 — Queensland Parliament — Assistant Electorate Officer
**S:** Injured worker at a client site (4cRisk) with a disputed workers compensation claim; employer, insurer, rehabilitation provider and medical specialist all had conflicting timelines and requirements.
**T:** Coordinate communication and documentation across all parties to prevent the case from stalling and ensure the worker's rehabilitation proceeded.
**A:** Created a single case-tracking log accessible to all authorised parties; set weekly check-in milestones with each stakeholder; escalated proactively when deadlines were at risk.
**R:** Claim resolved within the agreed rehabilitation timeline; worker returned to work.
**Reflection:** Stakeholder liaison fails when each party believes someone else owns the next action. I now always confirm ownership explicitly at the close of every meeting or call.
**Best for questions about:** managing multiple stakeholders; conflict or competing interests; coordination and follow-through; advocacy.

### [Workforce Scheduling / Roster] The 150-Worker Allocation
**Source:** Report #003 — QCS Woodford — Roster Officer
**S:** Thiess/TrackStar Alliance: single administrative office supporting a construction workforce of 150+ across multiple project sites operating continuously.
**T:** Coordinate inductions, compliance certification, return-to-work activities, and workforce records across site managers with competing scheduling demands.
**A:** Built and maintained tracking registers; liaised with site managers on workforce allocations and certification status; managed scheduling conflicts by escalating early and maintaining a master records view across all sites.
**R:** Zero non-compliance findings across three major safety audits; all site certification requirements met throughout the project delivery period.
**Reflection:** Would have implemented a dedicated roster or workforce management system from day one — the Excel-based approach was reliable at that scale, but purpose-built tools would have made cross-site visibility faster and reduced manual effort.
**Best for questions about:** roster or workforce management experience; coordinating large teams; working in a 24/7 or operational environment; administration under compliance requirements.

### [Financial Accuracy / Timesheet] The Reconciliation Run
**Source:** Report #003 — QCS Woodford — Roster Officer
**S:** At Thiess, invoice, purchase order and reconciliation processing for a large construction workforce and vendor base with tight financial reporting deadlines.
**T:** Ensure all financial records were accurate, matched source documents, and were processed within compliance and project reporting requirements.
**A:** Cross-checked every figure against source documents before processing; built a discrepancy log and flagged items for manager review before they became payment errors; maintained clear records for audit trail.
**R:** Accurate financial records across the entire tenure; no overpayment, underpayment or reconciliation errors raised.
**Reflection:** Award and pay compliance in a rostering context is the same discipline applied to a different set of rules. Learning the specific entitlements early is the priority — the accuracy system is transferable.
**Best for questions about:** timesheet validation; financial accuracy; award or compliance awareness; attention to detail.

### [Autonomy / Reliability] The Solo Office
**Source:** Report #002 — Queensland Parliament — Assistant Electorate Officer
**S:** At Thiess, the site manager was frequently absent during project site visits, leaving the office to run independently for days at a time. Later, operated as a sole business owner with no backup staff for nine years.
**T:** Maintain operational standards and stakeholder responsiveness without day-to-day direction.
**A:** Developed reference checklists and documented decision escalation frameworks so I could determine clearly what I could resolve independently versus what required the principal's judgement. Wrote up "how we do things" procedures so nothing lived only in my head.
**R:** Consistent feedback from managers and clients that operations ran smoothly regardless of management presence.
**Reflection:** Autonomy requires documented standards. Writing things down is not bureaucracy — it is how a reliable person remains reliable even when circumstances change.
**Best for questions about:** working independently; reliability; managing an office alone; initiative; the self-employed return to employment question.
