## How it works

1. Every time `/career-ops oferta` generates Block F (Interview Plan), new STAR+R stories get appended here
2. Before your next interview, review this file — your stories are already organized by theme
3. The "Big Three" questions can be answered with stories from this bank:
   - "Tell me about yourself" → combine 2-3 stories into a narrative
   - "Tell me about your most impactful project" → pick your highest-impact story
   - "Tell me about a conflict you resolved" → find a story with a Reflection

### [Design Org Building] Building a 10-Person Design Function from Scratch
**Source:** Reports #004, #005, #006, #014, #030 (Smartsheet, AlphaSense, Medallion, Roo, User Interviews)
**S:** Joined Bark.com when design function was 2 people, no system, no research practice, with a mandate to ship AI features that drive revenue.
**T:** Build a world-class design org while shipping AI features that generate measurable business outcomes.
**A:** Hired 8 designers in 18 months, built design system from scratch, launched AI literacy programs for non-technical stakeholders, established research function with AI feature evaluation frameworks, embedded designers in squads.
**R:** 2→10 team, 100% retention during 4x growth, 50% faster feature deployment, 12+ concurrent AI experiments supported, 63% YoY revenue growth.
**Reflection:** The system comes before scale — build the infrastructure before you hire. Retention in hypergrowth requires clear growth paths and genuine investment in people, not perks.
**Best for questions about:** "Tell me about building a design team" / "How do you scale a design function?" / "Tell me about a time you grew an org from scratch" / "Leadership style"

### [Founder Credibility] CaseLab — Zero to PMF to Acquisition in 10 Months
**Source:** Reports #008, #009, #018, #020, #021 (Vercel, Arize, Anthropic roles)
**S:** Identified a market gap: designers spent days manually compiling portfolio case studies from scattered artifacts. No good AI solution existed.
**T:** Build and ship an AI product from zero that compresses the workflow and preserves human creative control.
**A:** Designed end-to-end: LLM orchestration pipeline, UX with human-in-the-loop checkpoints, transparent AI reasoning, edit-override patterns. Solo design + engineering. Go-to-market included direct user acquisition.
**R:** PMF achieved; 500+ users; acquired in 10 months. Multi-day workflow compressed to under 60 minutes.
**Reflection:** Speed of iteration matters more than perfection at zero-to-one. The human control points (where users can override AI) were the critical design decision — users reported "it sounds like me."
**Best for questions about:** "Describe entrepreneurial thinking" / "Zero-to-one experience" / "Tell me about a project you shipped solo" / "AI and human creative collaboration"

### [Enterprise Scale] Workday — 2M+ User Enterprise Platform Redesign
**Source:** Reports #004, #005, #006 (Smartsheet, AlphaSense, Medallion)
**S:** Workday's internal enterprise platform served 2M+ users globally with outdated UX and fragmented workflow automation. Process was deeply manual in a compliance-heavy environment.
**T:** Lead systems redesign affecting the full user base while managing 100+ internal stakeholders and building a measurement framework.
**A:** Architected workflow automation UX, built modular design system, managed exec-level change management, established performance framework tracking task completion, adoption, and satisfaction.

### [Human-AI Control] Designing AI Capability Without Replacing Human Agency
**Source:** Report #021 (Anthropic Education Labs)
**S:** CaseLab automated a creative process. Users' biggest fear: "If AI writes my case study, is it still mine?"
**T:** Design an AI workflow that feels like a powerful creative assistant — not a ghostwriter or a replacement.
**A:** Built human-in-the-loop checkpoints at every meaningful creative decision point. AI suggests structure and language; human approves all narrative choices. Made AI contributions transparent and editable at every step.
**R:** Users reported feeling "more creative, not replaced." 500+ users. Acquired in 10 months. The acquisition validated product-market fit; the user feedback validated the design philosophy.
**Reflection:** Preserving human agency in AI workflows is not a design constraint — it's the product. The UX of AI systems should make the AI invisible while keeping the human in control of what matters.
**Best for questions about:** "AI and human capability" / "How do you design for AI trust?" / "Design philosophy" / "AI ethics in product"

### [Research as Strategy] Founding a Research Practice that Changes How Decisions Are Made
**Source:** Reports #004, #005, #030 (Smartsheet, AlphaSense, User Interviews)
**S:** Bark.com had no research function. 15+ AI feature experiments were running with no clear framework for measuring design effectiveness. Decisions were made on gut feel.
**T:** Build research capability from scratch and embed it in product decision-making — changing how resources are allocated, not just informing individual features.
**A:** Founded the research function, established evaluation frameworks for AI features, ran 15+ hypothesis tests across usability testing, analytics, and business KPIs. Built hybrid measurement model (qualitative + performance + business metrics). Embedded in sprint and planning cycles.
**R:** 40% reduction in failed AI experiments; research embedded in quarterly planning; 8 AI features optimized; framework adopted across the org. 100% of resource allocation decisions shifted to evidence-based methodology.
**Reflection:** Research is most powerful when it changes how decisions are made, not just what decisions are made. The framework had more leverage than any individual finding.
**Best for questions about:** "Research experience" / "Evidence-based design" / "How do you influence without authority?" / "How do you measure design impact?"

### [Agentic UX] Designing for Invisible Complexity — Fetchd.ai
**Source:** Report #009 (Arize AI)
**S:** Fetchd.ai's pipeline involves multi-marketplace API connections, LLM-generated structured pages, schema.org data, and AI referral tracking — genuinely complex.
**T:** Design an interaction model where none of this complexity is visible to non-technical marketplace sellers.
**A:** Progressive disclosure model: users see "connect your shop → AI does the rest." Every complex pipeline step abstracted into a status indicator and clear outcome. Built failure recovery flows and trust signals.
**R:** Marketplace sellers with zero technical background successfully activate and maintain the full AI pipeline. Complex = invisible to users.
**Reflection:** The best agentic UX is the one that doesn't make you aware of the AI. Complexity is a design failure, not a feature.
**Best for questions about:** "Agentic UX design" / "Designing for non-technical users" / "AI workflow design" / "Interaction design philosophy"
