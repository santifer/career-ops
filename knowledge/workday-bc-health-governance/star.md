# Workday BC Health Governance — STAR Framework (Interview/External)

## SITUATION

British Columbia's health system — consisting of seven regional health authorities, 300+ hospitals, and 220,000+ employees — operated on fragmented enterprise systems. Different health organizations used different payroll, HR, and financial platforms, creating inefficiencies, data inconsistencies, and operational friction.

The strategic initiative: Unify all seven health authorities on a single Workday platform. This was transformational change at provincial scale — affecting every hospital, every HR team, every payroll operation, and every employee across the entire BC health system.

The challenge was not purely technical. Seven independent health organizations had different cultures, priorities, and political interests. Provincial government had its requirements. Hospitals needed to continue operating without disruption. And 220,000+ employees needed to transition to new systems with minimal operational impact.

Governance, alignment, and program discipline were more critical than technology.

---

## TASK

Lead the Transformation Program and Governance Office workstream to:

- **Design governance model** — Create decision-making structure enabling seven independent organizations to act as unified entity
- **Establish program office** — Set up infrastructure managing 250+ resources across geographically dispersed teams
- **Manage executive alignment** — Keep C-suite stakeholders aligned on progress, risks, and decisions despite competing organizational interests
- **Ensure program execution** — Deliver platform migration at scale without disrupting ongoing healthcare operations
- **Manage organizational change** — Support 220,000+ users through transition to new system with minimal friction

---

## ACTION

### Governance Model & Program Structure

**Designing Decision Architecture**
- Designed governance model enabling seven health authorities to coordinate while maintaining organizational autonomy
- Established clear decision rights: Who decides? How are conflicts resolved? What decisions go to steering committee?
- Created escalation paths for issues that couldn't be resolved at working levels
- Built executive steering committees with senior leadership from health authorities and provincial government
- Established regular executive reporting cadence keeping leadership informed without creating decision bottlenecks

**Program Office Infrastructure**
- Stood up Transformation Program Office coordinating 250+ resources
- Organized work streams around technical, organizational, and change management domains
- Built project management discipline: timelines, status reporting, resource tracking, risk management
- Established cross-organization communication channels enabling coordination while respecting organizational boundaries

### Executive Engagement & Alignment

**Steering Committee Management**
- Structured steering committees with clear agendas, decision items, and escalation protocols
- Provided transparent risk reporting: issues, mitigation strategies, impact on timeline/budget
- Made recommendations with supporting analysis enabling informed decision-making
- Built executive confidence in program execution and governance discipline

**Stakeholder Relationship Management**
- Engaged health authority leadership to understand organizational priorities and constraints
- Managed political dynamics effectively: seven organizations had different interests; bridged gaps without forcing alignment
- Built trust through consistent communication, follow-through on commitments, and transparent escalation
- Ensured executive stakeholders felt heard and that their concerns were addressed

### Program Execution & Resource Coordination

**Resource Management at Scale**
- Coordinated 250+ program resources across seven health organizations
- Managed capacity planning: who is doing what, when, with what dependencies
- Tracked resource utilization and reallocated where needed to maintain schedule
- Built shared services model enabling efficiency at scale

**Cross-Organization Coordination**
- Facilitated coordination between seven health authorities
- Established shared standards and common processes where possible; allowed regional customization where necessary
- Managed dependencies between organizations
- Built consensus-based decision-making for issues affecting multiple organizations

### Organizational Change & User Transition

**Change Management Strategy**
- Developed change management approach for 220,000+ users
- Addressed organizational culture dimensions: different health organizations had different change readiness
- Created executive sponsorship model: C-suite leaders championed change within their organizations
- Managed communications: user education, training, ongoing support

**Training & Support Infrastructure**
- Designed training programs adapted for different user populations (clinicians, administrators, HR staff, payroll teams)
- Built support infrastructure: help desk, super users, knowledge base, feedback mechanisms
- Managed transition from legacy systems with minimal disruption to ongoing healthcare operations
- Provided ongoing support addressing questions, issues, and resistance to change

### Risk Management & Program Discipline

**Proactive Risk Management**
- Identified risks across technical, organizational, and political domains
- Developed mitigation strategies: what are we going to do to reduce this risk?
- Escalated risks transparently to executive steering committee
- Maintained active risk monitoring throughout program lifecycle
- Adjusted program plan when risks materialized; managed contingencies effectively
