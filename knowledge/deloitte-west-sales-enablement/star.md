# STAR Framework: Deloitte West Sales Enablement Platform

**Interview Context:** External audience (startup, corporate, investor); demonstrates product thinking, leadership impact, architectural depth, measurable business outcomes

---

## SITUATION

I inherited a problem that was costing Deloitte West millions in lost velocity and margin. The region was managing a $1.5B pipeline entirely through manual, reactive processes — pulling Excel downloads from our CRM weekly and spending 4-6 hours on analysis per cycle. We had no visibility into trends, no predictive capability, and our sales teams were flying blind. Leadership knew something was broken, but nobody had built the right solution to fix it.

On top of that, our data was only 3 years deep. We had a decade of sales patterns right there, untapped, that could have informed strategy and driven better decision-making.

**The stakes:** For a professional services firm operating at scale, losing 4-6 hours per analyst per week on manual reporting is a serious drag on profit margin. And from a competitive standpoint, we were moving slower than the market.

---

## TASK

I took ownership of a complete redesign: architecting, building, and deploying an end-to-end intelligence platform that would transform the West region's ability to make data-driven decisions in real time.

The scope was significant. This wasn't just a dashboard — it was a full platform with multiple interconnected layers:
- A daily data ingestion system to replace weekly manual uploads
- Expansion of our historical dataset (3 years → 10 years) while actually *improving* system performance
- Custom ETL pipelines to cleanse and structure data for analysis
- A comprehensive BI layer with standardized KPIs
- Predictive analytics to enable proactive, intelligent team positioning
- Gamification mechanics to drive behavioral change
- Automated notification systems to keep teams synchronized

I built the entire platform myself from architecture through deployment.

---

## ACTION

**Data Infrastructure:**
I designed and implemented a daily API-driven data ingestion pipeline that pulled directly from our CRM. This alone was a 7x improvement in data freshness. But the bigger win was structuring the backend to handle 10 years of historical data — a 3.3x volume increase — while actually reducing query latency by 50%. Most systems would have buckled under that data load. We optimized the architecture to handle it elegantly.

**Analytics & BI Layer:**
I built a comprehensive KPI framework across the entire sales funnel: pipeline value, opportunity status, conversion rates, profitability, time-to-close, and cross-sell patterns. Power BI dashboards connected directly to the platform, giving leadership real-time visibility into pipeline health at multiple levels of granularity (by customer, product, industry, pursuit leader).

**Intelligence Engine:**
This is where the platform became strategic. I built predictive models that analyzed 10 years of historical trends across customer industries and product lines to identify which products each customer would likely purchase next. This allowed us to position "tiger pursuit teams" proactively — not reactively chasing warm leads, but positioning ourselves *before* customers even knew they had a problem. That kind of intelligence multiplier is what separates reactive sales organizations from proactive ones.

**Account Categorization & Targeting:**
I implemented a classification framework (anchor accounts, phase zero, net new) that used historical conversion data to break down annual goals into realistic percentage allocations by segment. This replaced guesswork with data-driven targeting.

**Gamification & Behavioral Design:**
I integrated leaderboards and competition mechanics tied directly to dashboard metrics. The psychology here is subtle but powerful — when pursuit leaders see their performance ranked in real time against peers, and they see that performance is driven by concrete metrics, collaboration increases. We measured a 3% average increase in pursuit leader sales within 3 months of launch.

**Automation & Orchestration:**
The entire pipeline was event-triggered. Once API ingestion completed successfully, ETL would execute automatically. Reports that used to take 4-6 hours were now running in under 5 minutes, with zero human intervention. I automated weekly status emails to pursuit teams with embedded reports and live dashboard links.

**Strategic Feedback Loop:**
I connected the entire platform back to Deloitte's global product strategy. Sales data directly informed which products the market was buying, which fed into product investment decisions for our Asset Center of Excellence. I used this system as the intelligence backbone for my parallel role as O&G Portfolio Lead.

- Reduced reporting cycle time from 4-6 hours to under 5 minutes
