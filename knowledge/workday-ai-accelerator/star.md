# Workday Global AI Accelerator — STAR Framework (Interview/External)

## SITUATION

Workday was rapidly becoming an AI-powered company, integrating AI capabilities across HR, finance, planning, and talent management — positioning itself as the enterprise system of record for People, Money, and Agents. But Workday's Professional Services organization wasn't positioned to capitalize on these capabilities.

The problem was multi-dimensional:

- **Sales Challenge:** Services didn't have AI offerings to sell. Customer conversations about AI ROI were happening but Professional Services couldn't close them into engagements.

- **Consultant Capability Gap:** 4,000+ consultants were trained on Workday platform, but almost none had deep AI or agentic expertise. The new Agent System of Record (ASOR), custom agent development, MCP interoperability — this required entirely new skill sets.

- **Customer Adoption Gap:** 80% of enterprise AI projects fail to deliver measurable value. MIT estimates 95% of GenAI pilots fail to deliver P&L impact. Customers had licensed AI capabilities sitting idle — they needed advisory on strategy, operating models, governance, and activation.

- **Product Feedback Loop Gap:** Product teams were building agent capabilities (ASOR, Sana, Agent Gateway, MCP) but weren't receiving enough feedback from real customer deployments. Early Adopter customers were hitting technical barriers (3P agent registration, security model complexity, authentication issues) with no services team to bridge the gap.

The opportunity: Build the team that would define, sell, enable, and deliver AI across the entire Professional Services organization — and close the gap between Workday's AI vision and customer production reality.

Selected as one of 20 globally (from ~100 nominees, 2 per vertical across EMEA, APAC, US, Canada) to be founding member of Global AI Accelerator. My specific mandate extended beyond the general team charter because of my unique combination of technical depth and strategic consulting skills:

### Authored the AI Acceleration Service — The Team's Primary Tactical Motion

Designed and authored the definitive operational blueprint for the team's primary customer engagement: a "Strategy-as-Implementation" motion that moves customers from AI curiosity to production utility in 60 days.

**Phase 1: "Searchlight" — Outcome-Led Prioritization (Weeks 1-2)**

Created a discovery methodology that doesn't ask what's broken — it shows what's possible:
- **AI Inventory Dashboard:** Live-tenant audit mapping every licensed SKU to active vs. inactive AI features using a Green/Yellow/Grey classification system. Secured sandbox access for unfiltered configuration analysis.
- **Top 5 Positive Outcomes:** Developed an Accelerator Scoping Matrix plotting Effort-to-Deploy against Economic Benefit. Presented outcomes using Metis Strategy consulting frameworks as business impact, not technical features.
- **Pre-packaged ROI Framework:** Built centralized Master Repository with off-the-shelf metrics and AI-generated ROI formulas. Each recommended capability came with validated benchmarks and customer-specific success metrics.
- **Scalable Data Processing:** Designed workflow (potentially AI-model-assisted) to automate the tenant analysis and generate visual priority presentations at scale.

**Phase 2: "Strike" — Rapid Activation (Weeks 3-8)**

Designed the production activation methodology:
- Three-bucket approach: Quick Flips (GenAI features), Agentic Onboarding (ASOR, Sana Core), Heavy Models (ML capabilities like Payroll Anomaly Detection)
- "2-in-the-Box" staffing: every Workday resource paired with customer counterpart
- Variable staffing logic with dynamic specialist allocation on 2-4 week notice
- Week-by-week rhythm: sandbox prototype → production deployment → value hand-off with "Finish Line" guides

**Commercial Model:** $15K-$25K paid discovery with 100% roll-forward credit toward activation engagement. "Value Story" discounts for signed referenceability agreements.

**First pilot:** Northeast Georgia Health System — premiere engagement timed to 2026 R1 release (mid-March).

### Architected Team Agile Operating Model (with Greg Foster)

When the 20-person team launched, everyone was independently exploring AI with no coordination, shared priorities, or visibility. Partnered with Greg Foster (Global Head of AI) to design and implement the team's agile operating model from scratch:

- **Smartsheet Backlog System:** Centralized backlog tracking 70+ items across 25 data fields (description, owner, status, effort estimates as story points, offering alignment, priority classification, weekly updates, days remaining). Formal status lifecycle (Intake → New → Backlog → In Progress → Done → Deferred → Blocked → Rejected). Covers both customer engagements and internal enablement/investment work.
- **Executive Dashboard:** Real-time leadership reporting on team status, work distribution across offerings, effort allocation, and progress against priorities.
- **Kanban Board:** Visual work-in-progress management for weekly team standups.
- **AI COE Intake Process:** Formal qualification process for new work items — ensuring alignment with team priorities and the correct service offering category before entering the active backlog.
- **Swim Lane Specialization Model:** Designed a Focus Distribution Sheet assigning team members across 14+ AI capability areas and 10+ service offerings with regional expert designations (APJ/EMEA/Americas). Jon assigned as Americas expert for ASOR and Agent Builders. Design principle: specialists become SMEs, upskill themselves, then train the rest of the team — "rising tides raise all boats."
- **Weekly Operating Cadence:** Defined rhythm for backlog review, standup, and cross-training with documented status updates per item.

Impact: Transformed the team from an uncoordinated "free-for-all" into operational excellence — demonstrating the ability to build an AI professional services team at enterprise level from the ground up.
