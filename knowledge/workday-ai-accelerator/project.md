# Workday Global AI Accelerator — Project Details

## Overview

Founding member of Workday's Global AI Accelerator — an elite, strategically critical team tasked with defining and scaling AI capabilities across the entire Professional Services organization. Selected as one of only 20 globally (from approximately 100 nominees across EMEA, APAC, US, Canada) based on demonstrated impact in Healthcare practice AI innovation work. The Global AI Accelerator operates at enterprise scale, encompassing customer-facing advisory, internal capability building, product incubation, core product development, and the team's primary operational motion: moving customers from AI curiosity to production utility.

**Duration:** Q4 2025 to present (Q1 2026)
**Role:** Founding Member & ASOR/3P Agent Development Expert, Global AI Accelerator
**Organization:** Workday Professional Services — Global AI Accelerator
**Team Scope:** 20-person founding team; expanded significantly since establishment

## Business Context

Workday Professional Services recognized that AI was becoming core to competitive positioning and value delivery. However, AI adoption was uncoordinated across practices — 80% of enterprise AI projects fail to deliver measurable value, and MIT estimates 95% of GenAI pilots fail to deliver P&L impact. The Global AI Accelerator was established to:

- Define and launch the complete portfolio of AI service offerings
- Build internal AI capabilities enabling 4,000+ consultants to deliver at scale
- Incubate new AI-powered products and features (including agentic solutions)
- Create enablement and training infrastructure for the entire consultant base
- Lead Workday's positioning as the enterprise AI platform for People, Money, and Agents
- Drive the "Success Cycle" — moving past test drives and POCs into live customer tenants to deliver demonstrable, referenceable value

This was not a single-function team. The Accelerator was designed to work across product, sales, customer success, and consulting to create end-to-end AI capabilities.

Standardized AI Agent Change Management Framework included in every engagement: Prepare (stakeholder alignment, workflow understanding, data readiness, success definition) → Enable (communication strategy, leadership alignment, manager readiness, support readiness) → Activate (launch coordination, early adoption support, usage monitoring, communication reinforcement) → Optimize (adoption insights, operational improvements, sustainment planning, expansion opportunities).

Standardized post-engagement framework:
- **Implement & Launch (Now):** Deploy agents for priority use cases, ensure configurations/security/data access in place, begin measuring impact immediately

**Offering Evolution: v1 → v2**

The v1 was piloted at Merck as a single-day on-site discovery workshop with three custom workstreams. Based on field findings from the inaugural engagement, Jon worked with the service offering team to iterate the offering into the current v2:

| Dimension | v1 (Merck Pilot) | v2 (Current) |
|-----------|-----------------|--------------|
| **Format** | Single-day on-site workshop | Standardized 4-week engagement |
| **Scope** | Discovery-focused — identifying use cases and building roadmaps | Activation-focused — configuring and enabling agents in tenant |
| **Structure** | Three custom workstreams | Defined Agent Library with per-agent working sessions |
| **Duration** | 1 day + undefined post-workshop timeline | 8-10 hours over 4 weeks with clear milestones |
| **Change Management** | None | Full framework: Prepare → Enable → Activate → Optimize |
| **Success Metrics** | Use case count as primary metric | Per-agent baselines with adoption, resolution, and topic performance KPIs |
| **ASOR Role** | Not central | Foundation — ASOR configuration is the starting point |
| **Post-Engagement** | Undefined roadmap delivery timeline | Three-horizon framework (Now → Near → Next) |
| **WSP Support** | None | Agent Quick Cards, learning paths, FAQs, Slack channels |
| **Product Interlock** | Ad-hoc feedback | Structured monthly feedback, Agent iBOM, Launches tracker |

Key v1 findings that drove v2 structural changes:
- Undefined post-workshop timeline risk → formalized 4-week structure with milestones
- 81-use-case volume stressed original format → improved convergence and time-boxing
- Platform selection gap (Sana vs. Flowise vs. Extend) → clarified Agent Library with defined deployment paths
- Pre-workshop alignment gaps → standardized Prepare phase with tenant assessment
- Need for agent-specific depth → individual agent working session templates

### Operational Excellence — Agile Framework & Team Governance (Jon's contribution, with Greg Foster)

When the Accelerator team launched, 20 people were all highly motivated to learn AI but operating independently with no coordination, shared priorities, or visibility into what the team was collectively working on. Jon architected the team's agile operating model from the ground up — in collaboration with Greg Foster (Global Head of AI, Jon's manager) — transforming the team from a "free-for-all" state into operational excellence.

**Agile Backlog System (Smartsheet)**

Designed and implemented a centralized backlog in Smartsheet that serves as the team's single source of truth for all work — both customer engagements and internal enablement/investment activities. The backlog tracks:
- **25 data fields per item:** Row ID, Description, Detailed Description, Requestor, Owner(s), Backlog Status, Total Effort Estimate (story points — 1 point = can get done in a few hours), Activity Type, Weekly Update, Latest Comment, Status Last Updated, Offering alignment, Priority classification (Must Have / Should Have / Could Have), Impact assessment, Contributors, External Points of Contact, Start/End Dates, Skillset Needed, Workday Org Involvement, Overall Status (Green/Yellow/Blue), Completed Date, Days Remaining
- **Status lifecycle:** 1. Intake → 2. New → 3. Backlog → 4. In Progress → 5. Pending → 6. Done → 7. Deferred → 8. Blocked → 9. Rejected
- **Activity types tracked:** Investment/Enablement (team capability building), Customer Engagement (billable), Admin (operational)
- **Offering alignment:** Each backlog item maps to one or more of the 12 AI service offerings or portfolio-level initiatives

As of March 2026, the backlog tracks 70+ items across all team workstreams — from customer engagements (Merck, Home Depot, Accenture) to investment/enablement work (AI Data Readiness agent, MCP EA Program, internal VCR use cases) to operational items (backlog management, tenant access, SFDC permissions, team titles).

**Executive Dashboard**

Created an executive-level dashboard providing leadership with real-time visibility into team status, including work distribution across offerings, status breakdowns (Green/Yellow/Red), effort allocation between customer work and investment/enablement, and progress against priorities. Designed for monthly leadership reporting on team productivity and capacity.

**Kanban Board**

Implemented a kanban board view of the backlog for weekly team standups, enabling visual work-in-progress management and real-time status tracking across all concurrent workstreams.

**AI COE Intake Process**

Established a formal intake process for new backlog items — qualifying whether proposed work aligns with team priorities and the right offering category before entering the active backlog. This prevents scope creep and ensures the team focuses on high-impact work aligned with the AI service portfolio.

**Weekly Operating Cadence**

Defined the team's weekly rhythm for backlog review, standup, and cross-training activities. Backlog items receive weekly status updates (documented in the "Weekly Update" field) creating a running log of progress and decisions.
