# Merck AI Activation Discovery Workshop — Project Details

## Overview

Inaugural customer engagement for the Global AI Accelerator's AI Activation offering. Jon was hand-selected as co-lead Engagement Manager and lead for the technical workstream — the first live deployment of the team's AI service offerings to an enterprise customer. Merck (a major global pharmaceutical company) was the customer, and the engagement served as the proving ground for the offering model that would scale to the initial 200-customer rollout.

**Role:** Co-Lead Engagement Manager + Technical Workstream Lead

## Business Context

The Global AI Accelerator had designed 12 AI service offerings but had not yet deployed any to a live customer. The AI Activation offering needed to prove it could: (1) work with hybrid teams and multiple agent workstreams in a single session, (2) demonstrate immediate tangible value on-site to build customer confidence, and (3) capture rich insights feeding both product roadmaps and future customer engagements. Merck was the inaugural customer — the results would determine how the offering scaled to 200+ customers.

---

## Strategic Innovation: Workshop Design

### Repositioning the Offering

Jon led the strategic repositioning of the AI Activation offering, moving from a traditional requirements-gathering approach to a highly interactive, outcomes-focused discovery model. The key innovation was organizing the workshop around **three agent-aligned workstreams** rather than a single monolithic session:

**1. Self-Service Workstream** — Sana Self-Service Agent activation and use case discovery
**2. Performance/BP Optimize Workstream** — Business process optimization through AI agents
**3. Custom Build Workstream** — Custom agent development using Sana Agent Builder, Sana Enterprise, Flowise, or Extend

This structure allowed teams to go deep on their specific use cases, identify business value with precision, and map requirements directly to agent capabilities and roadmaps.

### Two-Phase Workshop Format

**Morning — Breakout Sessions:**
Structured breakouts where teams independently identified and prioritized use cases. Each breakout captured: use case description, business value statement, requirements (~3 bullets per use case), priority assignment (H/M/L), and where known, mapping to agent skills roadmap.

**Afternoon — Executive Convergence:**
Executives participated in real-time cross-prioritization of all use cases surfaced across breakouts, with roadmap bucket assignment. This balanced thorough discovery with time constraints while maintaining executive visibility throughout.

### Stakeholder Alignment

Jon worked with stakeholders across sales, delivery, and product to design an engagement model that was both customer-centric and operationally efficient. The workshop included on-site and remote participants, Merck stakeholders, and Workday sales executives.

## Workshop Execution & Results

### Use Case Identification — 81 Use Cases

Across all three breakout sessions, the team collectively identified **81 use cases:**

- **Self-Service:** 55 use cases
- **Custom Build:** 21 use cases
- **Performance/BP Optimize:** 5 use cases

Each captured with business value statements, requirements, and priority assignments. This became the foundation for all downstream roadmap work.

### On-Site Agent Activation (Same-Day Value)

During the Self-Service breakout, Jon identified that Merck had been unable to configure the **Sana Self-Service Agent** and worked directly with the team to unblock them. This on-site activation enabled Merck to immediately begin advanced battle testing across their 55 identified Self-Service use cases **the same day** — demonstrating tangible value and building trust within the first hours of the engagement.

This was critical: it proved the AI Activation offering could deliver immediate, concrete results — not just a roadmap for future work.

### Product Alignment & Roadmap Feedback

Jon mapped use cases against existing agent skills and product roadmaps, identifying gaps where agent capabilities did not yet meet customer requirements. Established direct working relationships with Product teams to feed voice-of-customer insights into roadmap prioritization, positioning Merck as a valuable Early Adopter partner.

### Custom Build Platform Strategy

Through the Custom Build breakout, Jon worked with the Sana product team to evaluate 21+ custom use case scenarios and map each to the appropriate deployment path:

- **Sana Agent Builder** — for standard agent configurations
- **Sana Enterprise** — for enterprise-grade custom builds
- **Flowise** — for complex agentic workflows (Jon's domain expertise)
- **Extend** — for deep platform extensions

This exercise surfaced a **critical internal gap:** the go-to-market team lacked clear guidance on when to recommend each platform. Jon identified this as a scaling risk and initiated a CX enhancement initiative to address it before future customer deployments.

### Sales Opportunity Identification

Through Custom Build discussions, Jon identified a significant sales signal: **Merck is evaluating Sana Orchestrate** for orchestration use cases and has it on their short list for purchase. Coordinated dedicated follow-up sessions and worked with the sales team to position this opportunity properly given Merck's concurrent participation in the Sana Toast/Lighthouse program.

## Post-Workshop: Roadmap & Next Steps

### Current Status (Late March 2026)

The end goal is to deliver a comprehensive AI roadmap for Merck spanning current and future capabilities:

- Merck is completing internal stack ranking and priority validation across all 81 identified use cases
- Workday is working with Product to validate use case feasibility, confirm roadmap commitments, and address skill gaps across the three agent workstreams
- A Performance/BP Optimize EA onboarding opportunity has been identified; coordinating with the account team to get Merck onboarded
- Comprehensive roadmap to be built, reviewed, and delivered to Merck

### Roadmap Delivery Framework (from Next Steps Deck)

Post-workshop execution organized into three horizons:

- **Implement & Launch (Now):** Activate Workday-delivered agents within a scalable framework
- **Review & Adjust (Near, 1-3 Months):** Optimize configurations and expand activated use cases
- **Optimize & Scale (Next, 3+ Months):** Scale AI adoption across the enterprise

Merck's stated outcome: **"Empower Managers to take on processes that their HRBPs do today."**

Specific activation paths identified:
- **Performance Optimization:** Optimization Package for Employee Review Process (Performance Reviews, Goals, Feedback) using Performance Agent and BP Optimize Agent
- **MSS Optimization:** Streamline Manager Self Service (Staffing BPs, Orgs, Job Catalog) using Job Architecture Agent
- **ASOR & Sana Self-Service Agent** activation (already unblocked on-site)

### Risk Identification & Escalation

Jon identified and escalated a critical risk: **the timeline and effort required to move from workshop completion to finalized, actionable roadmap is currently undefined.** The path involves multiple interdependent workstreams:

- Merck must complete internal stack ranking across 81 use cases
- Workday must validate use case feasibility with Product and confirm roadmap commitments
- Comprehensive roadmap must be built, reviewed, and delivered
- Actioning the roadmap — who does what, in what sequence, on what timeline — adds another layer

**Recommended action:** Dedicated scoping session with stakeholders to define engagement runway, clarify ownership, and establish realistic timeline. Without this, the engagement risks extending indefinitely without a clear finish line.

## Offering Iteration & Scaling

### Lessons Learned → Offering Enhancement

Following Merck, Jon synthesized learnings in partnership with Kim, Covenant, and Kristen to create an iterative improvement roadmap:

- Refined pre-workshop prep process for better alignment on customer objectives and team composition
- Clarified platform selection criteria (Sana / Flowise / Extend) to enable field teams to make confident recommendations
- Developed stronger templates and frameworks for use case capture and business value estimation
- Improved afternoon convergence format to accommodate larger volumes (80+ use cases) while maintaining executive engagement
- Created clearer handoff processes between discovery and roadmap execution phases

### Scaling to Next Customers: Home Depot & Accenture

Jon has embedded the Merck lessons into the next iteration of the AI Activation offering, now being prepared for deployment with **Home Depot** and **Accenture**. Specific enhancements:

- Revised workshop structure accounting for Merck-scale volumes (80+ use cases) with clearer time-boxing
- Enhanced platform selection guidance embedded into the Custom Build breakout
- Improved roadmap templates distinguishing: immediate implementation, short-term roadmap items, and custom build opportunities
- Pre-workshop alignment sessions to set expectations around effort, timeline, and deliverables

Jon is actively advising on both the Home Depot and Accenture engagements to ensure consistency while remaining flexible to customer-specific needs.

### Impact on the v2 Agent Activation Service

Jon's Merck findings were directly embedded into the v2 Agent Activation Service — a fundamental redesign of the offering from a single-day discovery workshop to a standardized 4-week activation engagement. Working with the service offering team, the v2 includes:

- **Formalized 4-week structure:** Week 1 Kick-Off → Weeks 2-3 Activate → Week 4 Ongoing Adoption (directly addressing the undefined post-workshop timeline risk Jon escalated)
- **Defined Agent Library:** ASOR and Deployment Agent always included; Sana Self-Service, BP Optimize, Payroll, and Planning Agents available for customer selection (replacing the custom workstream approach)
- **Per-agent working sessions:** Individual templates with configuration guides, success baselines, skill definitions, and go-live checklists (addressing the need for agent-specific depth identified at Merck)
- **Standardized Prepare → Activate → Adopt methodology** with tenant assessment and use case review before customer sessions (addressing pre-workshop alignment gaps)
- **Change Management Framework:** Prepare → Enable → Activate → Optimize (not present in v1)
- **Per-agent success metrics with baselines:** Adoption, resolution, and topic performance KPIs (evolving from the use case count approach at Merck)
- **Three-horizon next steps model standardized:** Implement & Launch (Now) → Review & Adjust (1-3 Months) → Optimize & Scale (3+ Months)
- **Product interlock infrastructure:** Monthly feedback for Product deck, Agent iBOM, Launches SoT Tracker, First 200 Agent Feedback (formalizing the ad-hoc feedback from Merck)
- **WSP consultant support:** Agent Quick Cards with prerequisites, training paths, and gotchas for each agent
