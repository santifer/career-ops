# Merck AI Activation Discovery Workshop — STAR Framework

> **Framing:** EXTERNAL — for interviews, LinkedIn, resume. Can emphasize influence, strategic framing, and be strategic with metrics.

---

## SITUATION

Workday's Global AI Accelerator had designed 12 AI service offerings to move enterprise customers from AI curiosity to production utility — but none had been tested with a live customer. The first engagement would set the template for a 200-customer rollout. The customer: Merck, a Fortune 50 global pharmaceutical company. The stakes: prove that a structured AI discovery offering could deliver immediate value, surface actionable insights, and create a repeatable model — all in a single on-site workshop.

---

## TASK

I was hand-selected as co-lead Engagement Manager and technical workstream lead for this inaugural engagement. My mandate: design the workshop strategy, lead the on-site execution across three parallel agent workstreams (Self-Service, Performance/BP Optimize, Custom Build), deliver same-day tangible value, and synthesize everything into a model that could scale to the next 200 customers.

---

## ACTION

### Redesigned the Offering Model

I repositioned the AI Activation offering from a traditional requirements-gathering approach to an outcomes-focused discovery model. The key innovation was organizing around three agent-aligned workstreams instead of a single monolithic session — allowing teams to go deep on their specific domain while maintaining executive visibility through an afternoon convergence session with real-time cross-prioritization.

I worked across sales, delivery, and product stakeholders to design an engagement model that was both customer-centric and operationally efficient. Designed the discovery deck, defined the breakout objectives, and structured the flow from morning deep-dives to afternoon executive prioritization.

### Delivered Same-Day Activation

During the Self-Service breakout, I identified that Merck had been unable to configure the Sana Self-Service Agent and worked directly with the team to unblock them on-site. This wasn't on the agenda — I saw the blocker and fixed it in real time. Merck immediately began battle testing across their 55 identified Self-Service use cases that same day. Within the first hours, the engagement had already delivered concrete value, not just a roadmap for future work.

### Led Technical Discovery Across All Three Workstreams

Facilitated use case identification across all three breakouts, resulting in 81 total use cases (55 Self-Service, 21 Custom Build, 5 Performance/BP Optimize). Each captured with business value statements, requirements, and priority assignments. Mapped use cases against existing agent skills and product roadmaps, identifying gaps and establishing direct working relationships with Product teams to feed voice-of-customer insights into roadmap prioritization.

### Surfaced Strategic Gaps and Sales Signals

Through the Custom Build breakout, I evaluated 21+ custom use case scenarios with the Sana product team and mapped each to the appropriate deployment path (Sana Agent Builder, Sana Enterprise, Flowise, or Extend). This surfaced a critical internal gap: the go-to-market team lacked clear platform selection guidance. I identified this as a scaling risk and initiated a CX enhancement initiative to fix it before future deployments.

I also identified a significant sales signal: Merck is evaluating Sana Orchestrate for orchestration use cases. Coordinated dedicated follow-up sessions with the sales team to position this opportunity.

### Drove Risk Management and Governance

Identified and escalated a critical risk: the timeline from workshop completion to finalized roadmap delivery was undefined. Recommended a dedicated scoping session to define the engagement runway, clarify ownership, and establish realistic timelines — preventing indefinite engagement extension.

### Scaled the Model to Next Customers

After Merck, I worked with the service offering team to fundamentally evolve the offering from a single-day discovery workshop into a standardized 4-week Agent Activation Service. The v2 includes: a formalized 4-week timeline (addressing the undefined post-workshop delivery risk I escalated), a defined Agent Library with 6 GA agents and 4 on roadmap (replacing the custom workstream approach), per-agent working sessions with configuration guides and success baselines, a standardized Prepare → Activate → Adopt methodology, a Change Management Framework, per-agent success metrics, and a product interlock infrastructure for structured feedback to product teams. The offering now being deployed to Home Depot and Accenture — and scaling to 200 customers — was directly shaped by my Merck findings. I'm actively advising on both engagements.

## RESULT

### Workshop Outcomes
- **81 use cases** identified and prioritized across 3 agent-aligned workstreams in a single workshop day
- **Same-day agent activation** — unblocked Merck's Sana Self-Service Agent on-site, enabling immediate battle testing across 55 use cases
- **21 custom build scenarios** mapped to specific deployment paths (Sana Agent Builder, Sana Enterprise, Flowise, Extend)
- **Sales opportunity surfaced** — Merck evaluating Sana Orchestrate, dedicated follow-up sessions coordinated
- **Product feedback loop established** — voice-of-customer insights feeding directly into agent skills roadmap prioritization

### Strategic Impact
- **Proved the offering model works:** First live customer engagement validated the three-workstream discovery format. 81 use cases in one session — proving the format could handle enterprise-scale complexity
- **Set the template for 200-customer rollout:** Merck findings drove the v2 Agent Activation Service — fundamentally redesigned from single-day workshop to standardized 4-week engagement with defined Agent Library (6 GA agents + 4 on roadmap), formalized methodology, per-agent working sessions, and product interlock infrastructure. Now deploying to Home Depot and Accenture
- **Identified a scaling risk before it scaled:** The platform selection gap (when to recommend Sana vs. Flowise vs. Extend) would have caused inconsistent recommendations across every future Custom Build engagement. Caught it at Merck, initiated the fix
- **Demonstrated same-day value delivery:** The on-site agent activation proved that AI Activation isn't just a discovery exercise — it delivers concrete results on day one
- **Positioned Merck as EA partner:** Established direct product team relationships for ongoing voice-of-customer feedback

### Merck's Stated Outcome
> "Empower Managers to take on processes that their HRBPs do today."

Activation paths identified: Performance Optimization (Employee Review Process), MSS Optimization (Manager Self Service), ASOR & Sana Self-Service Agent activation.
