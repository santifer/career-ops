# Project Details: Deloitte Global Oil & Gas Portfolio Leadership

**Timeline:** February 2021 - August 2022
**Organization:** Deloitte Consulting LLP
**Role:** Global Product Portfolio Lead, Oil & Gas Practice
**Scope:** Product strategy, asset commercialization, global delivery scaling

---

## Context & Problem Statement

Deloitte's Oil & Gas practice was executing successful client engagements across the globe, but value creation was being left on the table. Custom solutions built for specific clients weren't being captured, productized, or scaled for reuse. This created several challenges:

1. **Value Leakage:** Innovations developed in live client work were siloed within individual engagements
2. **Delivery Redundancy:** Teams across regions were solving similar problems independently
3. **Revenue Untapped:** Opportunities to monetize proven solutions as productized offerings weren't being pursued
4. **Knowledge Fragmentation:** No systematic mechanism to identify, evaluate, and operationalize emerging digital innovations
5. **Geographic Isolation:** Six Member Firms across three geographies were operating independently with limited cross-regional capability sharing

The opportunity was significant: Deloitte had the raw material (successful client engagements), but lacked the infrastructure to transform it into scalable, monetizable products.

---

## Solution: Asset Center of Excellence Model

Jon was appointed Global Product Portfolio Lead and built a complete end-to-end system for identifying, productizing, and scaling asset innovations.

### Phase 1: Strategic Framework & Infrastructure

**Asset Definition & Classification:**
- Defined what constituted a reusable "asset" within the O&G practice (SAP-focused modules, digital solutions, process templates, accelerators)
- Established quality and completeness standards for asset production
- Created assessment criteria for commercialization potential

**Intake Process Design:**
- Built a formal innovation intake process: customer innovations from live engagements → strip out client-specific customization → identify reusable core → productize for scale
- Created evaluation framework to assess which innovations warranted productization investment
- Established governance and approval workflows

**Delivery Model Architecture:**
- Designed global delivery models to enable consistent asset implementation across geographies
- Built playbooks for offshore/nearshore team execution
- Created handoff protocols between presales (solution design) and delivery (implementation)

### Phase 2: Global Deployment & Scaling

**Geographic Expansion:**
- Deployed integrated delivery models across 3 geographies (North America, Europe, APAC) in first year
- Scaled across 6 Member Firms within Deloitte's federated structure

**Team Enablement:**
- Trained customer-facing consultants at 6 member firms on asset usage, capabilities, and customization
- Developed enablement curriculum covering asset architecture, business case development, and client positioning
- Created support structures for ongoing consultant enablement as new assets launched

**Offshore/Nearshore Strategy:**
- Established offshore and nearshore teams for backend development and asset maintenance work
- Built capacity planning framework to support asset roadmap execution
- Created quality assurance processes for globally distributed development

### Phase 3: Product Roadmap & Commercialization

**IT Delivery Roadmap:**
- Defined multi-year technology roadmap for asset development
- Prioritized asset investments based on market opportunity and client demand signals
- Sequenced asset releases to enable synergies and avoid delivery conflicts

**Monetization Strategy:**
- Positioned assets as productized offerings for O&G clients (not internal-only tools)
- Created commercial models for asset licensing, implementation services, and ongoing support
- Developed case studies and ROI frameworks for sales enablement

**Innovation Capture System:**
- Established systematic framework for capturing emerging digital innovations from client engagements
- Built feedback mechanisms for field teams to surface innovation candidates
- Created scoring system to evaluate innovation against commercialization criteria

### Phase 4: Asset Portfolio Management

**Intake & Evaluation:**
- Managed continuous intake of asset innovation candidates from successful client programs
- Assessed technical feasibility, market demand, and commercial viability
- Prioritized asset roadmap based on strategic fit and revenue potential

**Portfolio Optimization:**
- Monitored asset performance across geographies (usage rates, customer satisfaction, revenue)
- Made investment/sunset decisions based on market traction
- Maintained quality standards and consistent customer experience

**Cross-Firm Coordination:**
- Facilitated knowledge sharing between member firms on asset implementation best practices
- Coordinated global launch timelines to maximize market impact
- Managed version control and asset evolution across distributed teams

## Strategic Connections

### Link to Sales Enablement Work
The O&G Portfolio Leadership role was directly connected to Jon's parallel work on the West Sales Enablement platform. Sales pipeline data (from the Sales CoE dashboards) directly informed asset investment decisions:
- Which product modules were customers buying? → Investment priority signals

This created a virtuous cycle: data from sales operations informed product strategy, which was executed by the asset delivery team, creating new sales assets, which fed back into the pipeline visibility system.
