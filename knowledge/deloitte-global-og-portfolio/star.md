# STAR Framework: Deloitte Global Oil & Gas Portfolio Leadership

**Interview Context:** External audience (product leadership, startup founder, corporate strategy role); demonstrates product thinking at scale, global operations leadership, revenue impact, pattern recognition

---

## SITUATION

Deloitte's Oil & Gas practice was generating millions in client revenue every year, but we were leaving value on the table. Our consultants were building incredible solutions for specific clients — custom implementations, digital integrations, process innovations — and then those solutions were locked inside individual engagements. The knowledge wasn't being captured. The code wasn't being reused. The innovations weren't being scaled.

Meanwhile, six different Deloitte Member Firms across three geographies were solving similar problems independently. We had brilliant people doing brilliant work, but the organization wasn't structured to systematically harvest and amplify that intelligence.

**The scale of opportunity:** If we could take proven solutions from client engagements, strip out the client-specific customization, productize them, and deploy them across the global practice, we could unlock significant revenue and competitive differentiation. But nobody had built the infrastructure to make that happen.

I was brought in as Global Product Portfolio Lead to change that.

---

## TASK

I took ownership of building an end-to-end system for identifying, productizing, and scaling asset innovations across Deloitte's global Oil & Gas practice.

The scope included:
- Creating the strategic framework: what constitutes a reusable, monetizable asset?
- Building the intake process: how do we capture innovations from live client work?
- Designing global delivery models that could be deployed across 3 geographies and 6 member firms
- Building the commercial model: how do we monetize these assets to clients?
- Establishing the governance: how do we make investment decisions about which innovations to pursue?
- Scaling the enablement: how do we train 6 member firms' sales and delivery teams on new assets?

This wasn't a one-time project — it was building organizational infrastructure that would run continuously.

---

## ACTION

**Strategic Framework:**
I started by defining what we meant by an "asset." It couldn't be internal-only tools — these had to be client-facing products that Deloitte could monetize. I worked with the member firms to understand the landscape: we had SAP implementations, digital transformation solutions, data integration platforms, process automation accelerators. The question was: which of these had repeatable market demand?

**Intake Process:**
I designed a systematic innovation capture mechanism. Whenever a consultant completed a successful client engagement, there was now a formal process to evaluate that work: Is there a reusable core? What was client-specific? What could be abstracted into a repeatable solution? This was the critical bottleneck — without this process, innovations stay locked in individual engagements.

**Asset Definition & Evaluation:**
I built scoring criteria to assess which innovations were worth productizing. It wasn't just about technical elegance — it was about market opportunity, competitive differentiation, and revenue potential. We evaluated dozens of candidates and prioritized the highest-impact assets for commercial development.

**Global Delivery Model:**
Here's where the scaling happened. Instead of each member firm running their own asset delivery independently, I designed integrated delivery models that could operate consistently across 3 geographies. That meant defining standard processes, quality gates, handoff protocols, and support structures. I worked with member firm leadership to operationalize these models.

**Offshore/Nearshore Strategy:**
Delivering custom solutions for clients is expensive. Scaling global products requires smarter cost structures. I established offshore and nearshore teams to handle backend development, asset maintenance, and implementation support. This allowed us to scale delivery without proportionally scaling cost.

**Enablement at Scale:**
The hardest part isn't building the product — it's getting your entire organization to sell and deliver it consistently. I trained consultant teams across all 6 member firms on the assets: how they work, what their capabilities are, what problems they solve, how to position them to customers. I built sales enablement resources, ROI calculators, case studies, implementation playbooks.

**Commercial Integration:**
I connected the asset portfolio directly to the sales and delivery organizations. These weren't internal tools that some innovation team was forcing on unwilling consultants — they were legitimate products that could be positioned to customers and monetized directly. The assets became revenue line items.

**Continuous Innovation Loop:**
I built feedback mechanisms so that emerging innovations from new client engagements would feed back into the portfolio. Which assets are being reused most? Which are gaining traction in the market? Where are we seeing new demand signals? This data informed the next generation of asset investments.

## INTERVIEW ANGLES & TALKING POINTS

### For Product Leadership/CPO Roles:
"This project taught me how to build products inside a services organization. Services companies are actually *ideal* for product innovation — you're solving real customer problems every day, you have direct feedback loops, you understand market needs intimately. The trick is building the infrastructure to systematize that. I created an intake process that captured innovations from client work, an evaluation framework that prioritized them against market opportunity, and a delivery model that could scale them globally. The result was 6 member firms operating on a consistent playbook instead of independently."

### For Startup Founder Context:
"If you scale from 3 geographies to 6 member firms, you're essentially managing 6 different startup units inside a larger organization. Each has its own leaders, its own culture, its own way of doing things. I had to design systems that coordinated them without crushing autonomy. That's the highest-difficulty version of ops scaling — not just building systems for one organization, but building systems that work across federated organizations with competing priorities."

### For COO/Scaling Roles:
"Global scaling is about creating repeatable playbooks, not about centralized control. I built a delivery model that 6 different member firms could adopt without feeling like top-down mandate. The key was showing them that the playbook *enhanced* their capability, not restricted it. Member firms adopted it because they saw their consultants becoming more effective and their margins improving — not because they had to."

### For Technical Depth:
"The asset portfolio included SAP implementations, digital transformation platforms, data integration solutions, and process automation. These were real, complex products — not templates. The scaling challenge wasn't technical, it was organizational. How do you maintain quality and consistency when the same asset is being delivered by teams in three continents in different time zones?"

### For P&L Ownership:
"I didn't just build products — I built a business model. Assets weren't just internal efficiency plays. We monetized them directly to clients. The financial modeling informed which assets we invested in. We tracked utilization rates, margin profiles, and customer retention. This wasn't theory — it was operationalized as revenue-generating products."

### For Board/Investor Pitch:
"I took a fragmented organization — 6 member firms solving similar problems independently — and created a scalable, repeatable infrastructure for productizing client innovations. Within the first year, we had integrated delivery models running across 3 geographies, trained 6 member firms' sales and delivery teams, and established a systematic mechanism for continuous innovation capture. The commercial impact: repeatable products with better margins and faster customer time-to-value."
