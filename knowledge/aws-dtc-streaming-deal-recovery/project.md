# Project Details: AWS DTC Streaming Platform Deal Recovery

**Timeline:** 2022-2023 (mid-engagement rescue)
**Organization:** Amazon Web Services (Professional Services)
**Client:** Direct-to-Consumer Streaming Platform Company
**Role:** Strategic Account Manager & Executive Relationship Leader (deal rescue and re-engagement)
**Scope:** Engagement recovery, customer retention, contract re-negotiation

---

## Context & Problem Statement

AWS had an engagement with a streaming platform company that had fundamentally failed. The customer CEO wanted to build a DTC (Direct-to-Consumer) streaming platform — a deployable system that other organizations (universities, corporations, media companies) could license and deploy their own instance, add their own content, and monetize.

**The Engagement Failure:**
- **Initial Scope:** Architecture and build a multi-tenant SaaS streaming platform
- **Customer Expectations:** White-label deployable platform; customers should be able to stand up instances with minimal technical overhead
- **What Happened:** The previous AWS consulting team completely misunderstood the requirements and built something that didn't meet the customer's vision
- **Customer Impact:** Significant time invested, significant budget spent, deliverable that fell short of expectations
- **Relationship Status:** CEO was considering leaving AWS and engaging a competitor

**The Stakes:**
- **Immediate Risk:** Loss of a named customer and significant contract value
- **Long-Term Risk:** Negative reference and reputational damage in streaming/media market
- **Market Opportunity:** If recovered correctly, could expand into larger, longer-term engagement

---

## The Recovery Strategy

Jon was brought in specifically to save the relationship. His role was strategic and interpersonal — he was not the technical architect, but the relationship leader focused on understanding what the customer actually needed and building a path to resolution.

### Phase 1: Assessment & Relationship Repair

**Executive Understanding:**
- Met directly with customer CEO to understand dissatisfaction and expectations
- Listened to detailed explanation of what was wrong with the previous build
- Assessed gap between what was built and what was needed
- Evaluated customer's willingness to continue partnership vs. switching to competitor

**Internal Assessment:**
- Analyzed previous team's work and deliverables
- Identified specific areas where requirements were misunderstood
- Determined what could be salvaged vs. what needed to be rebuilt
- Assessed AWS's ability to deliver what the customer actually needed

**Relationship Foundation:**
- Established credibility with CEO through honest assessment and listening
- Acknowledged AWS's failure to properly understand requirements
- Committed to finding resolution (not defensive posturing)
- Demonstrated genuine interest in customer success vs. extracting value

### Phase 2: Requirements Redefinition

**Deep Dive with Customer:**
- Facilitated detailed conversations about customer's vision for the platform
- Documented what "multi-tenant deployable SaaS" actually meant in their context
- Identified functional requirements (content management, user auth, payment processing, analytics)
- Clarified non-functional requirements (scalability, white-label customization, deployment automation)
- Understood the target customer use cases (universities, nonprofits, corporate intranets)

**Gap Analysis:**
- Mapped previous build against actual requirements
- Identified specific gaps in architecture and functionality
- Assessed what could be enhanced vs. what needed complete redesign
- Created prioritized roadmap for addressing shortcomings

**Commercial Negotiation:**
- Proposed refund or credit for the failed work
- Negotiated terms for new engagement to address requirements properly
- Aligned on scope, budget, timeline, and success criteria
- Secured new contract that reflected actual scope

### Phase 3: Transition & Handoff

**New Team Onboarding:**
- Brought in new AWS delivery team with fresh perspective
- Transferred detailed requirements documentation from discovery phase
- Ensured continuity of CEO relationship and alignment
- Established communication cadence and escalation paths

**Delivery Transition:**
- Jon rolled off the engagement once new contract was secured
- New delivery team took over technical architecture and build
- Jon maintained advisory relationship with customer (available if needed)
- Tracked delivery progress and customer satisfaction
