# Workday Community of Practice — STAR Framework (Interview/External)

## SITUATION

As Workday Professional Services accelerated AI adoption, consultants across different practices began experimenting independently with AI tools. These experiments were happening organically and producing value — but without coordination, they remained isolated pilots.

The Healthcare practice recognized both an opportunity and a challenge:
- **Opportunity:** Grassroots experiments represented early signals of where AI could genuinely improve delivery
- **Challenge:** Without coordination, these efforts were fragmented, knowledge wasn't shared, and high-impact ideas might never be systematized or scaled

The key insight: The best ideas often come from frontline practitioners who understand customer problems and constraints. Rather than imposing AI from the top down, the practice needed a mechanism to surface bottom-up innovation, evaluate it, and feed the most promising ideas into a coordinated development pipeline.

---

## TASK

Establish and lead the AI Community of Practice to:

- **Create intake mechanism** — Provide simple, accessible way for consultants to surface AI experiments and ideas
- **Curate and prioritize** — Evaluate ideas against clear criteria; identify highest-impact opportunities
- **Connect to innovation pipeline** — Feed promising use cases to AI Innovation Council for development and scaling
- **Build community and knowledge** — Create shared forums, build vocabulary, share learnings across practice
- **Drive adoption of scaled solutions** — Ensure that Innovation Council solutions addressed real needs and were adopted widely

---

## ACTION

### Establishing the Community of Practice

**Intake Structure**
- Created regular forums (meetings, channels, async submission processes) for consultants to surface AI experiments
- Designed simple, low-friction proposal process — didn't require perfect documentation or formal business cases
- Built visibility into ongoing AI work across teams
- Made participation safe and encouraging, even for early-stage ideas

**Membership & Engagement**
- Engaged consultants, program leadership, and innovation team members
- Built broad participation across Healthcare vertical
- Created sense of shared ownership of AI adoption
- Positioned Community of Practice as legitimate path for ideas to reach leadership

### Use Case Evaluation and Curation

**Criteria-Driven Prioritization**
- Evaluated proposals against:
  - Magnitude of consultant pain point
  - Potential productivity improvement
  - Feasibility and effort to build solution
  - Scalability across programs and teams
- Prioritized highest-impact opportunities for escalation
- Provided clear feedback to Community on why ideas were accepted or declined

**Connection to Innovation Council**
- Escalated prioritized use cases to AI Innovation Council
- Communicated selection criteria and prioritization rationale back to Community
- Created transparency so consultants understood how ideas moved through pipeline
- Built feedback loop: when Innovation Council deployed solutions, Community knew the origin and impact

### Building Community and Culture

**Knowledge Capture & Sharing**
- Documented grassroots AI experiments and learnings from across practice
- Shared best practices and patterns
- Built common vocabulary for discussing AI in implementation context
- Created institutional knowledge about where AI creates genuine value

**Organizational Learning**
- Connected isolated experiments into coherent narrative about AI impact
- Shifted culture from "AI is experimental" to "AI is strategic capability we're systematizing"
- Built stakeholder understanding of AI roadmap and how it connected to their work

---

## RESULT

### Pipeline Integration

**Use Case Sourcing**
- Surfaced 10+ high-impact use cases to AI Innovation Council
- Community of Practice proposals became foundation of Innovation Council development pipeline
- Ensured Innovation Council focused on real, validated consultant pain points

**Adoption and Impact**
- Innovation Council assets built from Community proposals achieved 80% adoption on marquee programs
- 7% organization-wide reduction in consulting hours — success depended on Community's role in identifying real needs
- High adoption rate validated that Community had surfaced genuine pain points

### Organizational Impact

**Cultural Shift**
- Transformed Healthcare practice from scattered AI experiments to coordinated innovation pipeline
- Legitimized bottom-up innovation and experimentation
- Created shared ownership of AI adoption across practice
- Built bridge between frontline consultants and innovation strategy

**Knowledge and Capability**
- Documented and socialized learnings from grassroots AI experiments
- Built common frameworks for evaluating and prioritizing AI use cases
- Created institutional knowledge about where AI creates value in professional services delivery

### Systemic View

By establishing the Community of Practice, created complete innovation system:

```
Community of Practice (Intake)
    ↓
AI Innovation Council (Evaluation & Development)
    ↓
Practice Deployment (Scaling & Adoption)
    ↓
Feedback to Community of Practice
```

This systemic approach was more effective than either top-down mandate or uncoordinated experimentation.

## Why This Matters

This engagement demonstrates:

1. **Systems Thinking** — Recognized that bottom-up innovation needed to be coordinated; designed structure connecting grassroots to strategic initiative

2. **Organizational Design** — Built intake mechanism that was simple enough to encourage participation but rigorous enough to drive real prioritization

3. **Innovation Leadership** — Understood that best ideas often come from practitioners; created safe space for ideas to surface

4. **Stakeholder Engagement** — Maintained trust with both frontline consultants (safe space to surface ideas) and leadership (clear prioritization and ROI)

5. **Cultural Change** — Shifted practice mindset from "AI is something happening elsewhere" to "We're systematically adopting AI based on our needs and learning"

6. **Pipeline Thinking** — Recognized that innovation isn't a one-time event but a continuous intake-evaluate-deploy-learn cycle
