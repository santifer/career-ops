## C) Level and Strategy

**Level detected:** Mid EM. "2+ years EM" — first-line EM, directly managing engineers.

**Fergus's natural level:** Exact match on seniority. The mobile/stack gap is the primary risk.

**How to sell:**
- Lead with backend strength: "My team's core work was high-volume, distributed backend systems — the same foundation your backend engineers work on. I can be credible with them from day one."
- Address mobile proactively: "I've managed cross-functional teams including engineers in adjacent stacks I haven't written in myself. I set the standards, review the approach, and partner with senior engineers on technical direction — I don't need to be the best mobile dev in the room."
- Lead with hands-on credibility: "The JD says hands-on — I've maintained technical contributions throughout my EM tenure. I review architecture, contribute to system design, and still have opinions worth challenging."
- Lead with fintech affinity: "Payments reliability is exactly the kind of zero-tolerance engineering I've practiced — SLOs, monitoring before go-live, zero-incident production changes."

**If pushed on mobile gap:** "I'm honest about this: I haven't written Swift or Kotlin professionally. What I bring to a mobile team is strong backend architecture sense, a track record of zero-incident launches, and a coaching approach that develops engineers into senior contributors regardless of stack."
